Matryoshka Gaussian Splatting

Published: 2026-03-19 | Authors: Zhilin Guo, Boqiao Zhang, Hakan Aktas, Kyle Fogarty, Jeffrey Hu | Tags: cs.CV, cs.GR

Abstract

The ability to render scenes at adjustable fidelity from a single model, known as level of detail (LoD), is crucial for practical deployment of 3D Gaussian Splatting (3DGS). Existing discrete LoD methods expose only a limited set of operating points, while concurrent continuous LoD approaches enable smoother scaling but often suffer noticeable quality degradation at full capacity, making LoD a costly design decision. We introduce Matryoshka Gaussian Splatting (MGS), a training framework that enables continuous LoD for standard 3DGS pipelines without sacrificing full-capacity rendering quality. MGS learns a single ordered set of Gaussians such that rendering any prefix, the first k splats, produces a coherent reconstruction whose fidelity improves smoothly with increasing budget. To address this gap, we present NavTrust, a unified benchmark that systematically corrupts input modalities, including RGB, depth, and instructions, in realistic scenarios and evaluates their impact on navigation performance.
